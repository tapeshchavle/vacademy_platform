How Do Batteries

The chemistry hiding inside your phone

Two Halves, One Reaction
Anode (−)
Oxidation happens here
Loses electrons → released into wire
Usually zinc or lithium
ELECTRON FLOW
e⁻ → → →
Cathode (+)
Reduction happens here
Gains electrons → absorbed
Usually copper or carbon
How Current Flows
1
Chemical Reaction Starts
Zinc atoms at the anode begin to dissolve
2
Electrons Released
Free electrons travel through the external wire
3
Current Powers Device
Electron flow = electrical current = your phone turns on
4
Cathode Absorbs
Electrons arrive at the cathode, completing the circuit
Battery Types Compared

Energy density determines how long your device lasts. Lithium-ion dominates because it packs the most energy per kilogram.

Alkaline — cheapest, shortest life
NiMH — rechargeable, moderate
Li-ion — phones, laptops, EVs
